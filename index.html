About

FPAI-HPC 2025

The rapid growth of data generation technologies, AI advancements, and rising privacy concerns have driven research in federated and privacy-preserving AI. Federated Learning (FL) enables collaborative AI model training without direct data sharing, originally designed for edge and mobile devices with limited computational resources. While traditional FL focuses on consumer applications, optimizing for decentralized processing and personal device privacy, FL for High-Performance Computing (HPC) introduces a new paradigm. Unlike edge-based FL, HPC-based FL involves a smaller number of highly capable clients—such as supercomputing centers and cloud-scale AI infrastructure—with immense computational power and high-speed interconnects. This shift requires rethinking optimization, aggregation, and privacy strategies.

HPC-based FL opens new research directions, including federated pretraining and fine-tuning of large-scale AI models while preserving data sovereignty, scalable aggregation methods in multi-cluster HPC systems, and communication-efficient strategies to minimize synchronization bottlenecks. Security and privacy challenges, such as adversarial robustness and backdoor vulnerabilities, require new solutions tailored for HPC environments. Additionally, FL can enhance large-scale scientific computing applications in climate modeling, genomics, and physics simulations, while energy-aware federated AI strategies can optimize resource allocation for sustainability. This workshop aims to unite researchers across machine learning, HPC, and scientific communities to advance federated AI for HPC through collaboration and shared objectives.
